Coffee Day Dashboard

Data analytics excel project

  1. Home
  2. Coffee Day Dashboard

Introduction:

This project presents a comprehensive analysis of a coffee shop’s sales performance using Microsoft Excel, employing Pivot Tables, slicers, and charts to generate meaningful business insights. The dataset includes transactional data such as product types, categories, hours, weekdays, months, store locations, and sales figures. The primary objective is to uncover trends in customer behavior, product preferences, and time-based sales patterns to support data-driven decision-making.

Data Preparation:

  1. Data Loading:

    The dataset is loaded into Power Query for further analysis.

  2. Data Type Adjustment:

    Data types are modified to ensure consistency and accuracy in subsequent analyses.

  3. Column Removal:

    The "neighbourhood" column, containing only null values, is removed to streamline the dataset.

  4. Price Formatting:

    The price column is formatted as currency to enhance readability.

  5. Date Extraction:

    Year and month are extracted from the "last review date" column to facilitate temporal analysis.

Pivot Tables:

Pivot tables are created to summarize and visualize key insights:

  1. Total Sales and Footfall KPIs:

    Measures total revenue, total number of transactions (footfall), average bill per person, and average number of items per order..

  2. Hourly Quantity Ordered:

    Visualizes how sales volume fluctuates over business hours, highlighting peak times like 8 AM and 5 PM.

  3. Sales by Weekday:

    Shows consistent sales across weekdays, with a slight increase on Sundays, indicating weekend footfall boost.

  4. Sales by Product Category:

    Segregates total sales by categories such as Coffee, Tea, Bakery, Drinking Chocolate, and Coffee Beans.

  5. Top 5 Products by Sales:

    Highlights best-selling individual products including Barista Espresso and Brewed Chai Tea.

Measures/KPIs:

  1. Total Sales: ₹1,18,941.08.
  2. Total Footfall: 25,335.
  3. Average Bill/Person: ₹4.69.
  4. Average Order/Person: 1.44.

Pivot Charts:

Charts were created to visually represent insights from pivot tables:

  1. Bar Chart: Sales by weekday.
  2. Line Chart: Quantity ordered by hour.
  3. 3D Column Chart: Monthly sales comparison.
  4. Pie Chart: Product category-wise sales.
  5. Bar Chart: Top product types.
  6. Column Chart: Sales by store location.

Results:

  1. Peak Hour Sales:

    Most orders occur around 8 AM, confirming that the morning coffee rush is a significant driver of sales. This indicates the importance of morning staff scheduling and marketing.

  2. Top Store:

    Hell’s Kitchen outperforms other locations with highest revenue, suggesting effective operations or better location traffic.

  3. Category Leaders:

    Coffee dominates category sales (₹45,971.20), followed by Bakery and Drinking Chocolate. Product-wise, Barista Espresso is the top seller.

  4. Product Preferences:

    Drinks like Hot Chocolate and Brewed Herbal Tea also perform well, showing demand for non-coffee beverages.

  5. Consistent Weekday Revenue:

    Sales remain steady across weekdays, with a noticeable uptick on weekends, suggesting family or social visits.
