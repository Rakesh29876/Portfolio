Introduction

This project provides a comprehensive analysis of student academic performance using a dataset enriched with multiple features. It leverages essential Python libraries such as NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib, and Seaborn for data preprocessing, statistical analysis, and visualization. The goal is to uncover patterns in student achievement and identify key factors that influence academic success.

Objective

  • Analyze the distribution of student performance across various features such as gender, parental education, test preparation, and lunch type.
  • Investigate correlations between different subject scores (Math, Reading, Writing) to understand inter-subject performance relationships.
  • Identify demographic or socioeconomic factors that significantly impact student outcomes.
  • Explore trends in academic performance to support educational decision-making and targeted interventions.
  • Present actionable insights through data visualization to enhance interpretation and policy formulation in the education sector.

Methodology

  1. Data import.

    The student performance dataset was imported into the Jupyter Notebook environment using the pandas library.

  2. Data describe.

    This dataset comprises 366 entries and 36 columns, offering a detailed view of student academic performance. It includes key features such as gender, race/ethnicity, parental level of education, lunch type, test preparation course, and scores in math, reading, and writing. This rich dataset enables multi-dimensional analysis of the factors influencing student outcomes.

  3. Data Analysis.

    Distribution of parents education and student scores:

    Higher parental education correlates with higher student scores across subjects such as Math, Reading, and Writing.

    Distribution of Math Scores:

    The boxplot reveals whether the distribution is symmetrical or skewed. A skewed distribution might indicate performance imbalances, such as more students scoring on the lower or higher end.

    Distribuation of ethinic score:

    Student performance varies across different ethnic groups due to a combination of socioeconomic, cultural, and educational access factors. Analyzing the distribution of scores (Math, Reading, Writing) across ethnic groups.

Result

  1. The dataset includes information on student demographics, parental background, lunch type, test preparation, and academic scores in Math, Reading, and Writing.
  2. Several columns, such as 'EthnicGroup', 'ParentEduc', 'TestPrep', 'ParentMaritalStatus', 'PracticeSport', 'IsFirstChild', 'NrSiblings', 'TransportMeans', and 'WklyStudyHours', contain missing values, indicating potential areas for data imputation or further investigation.
  3. An 'Unnamed: 0' column was identified and subsequently removed from the dataset during the analysis.
