Introduction

Uber has become one of the most popular ride-hailing services worldwide, generating vast amounts of trip-related data every day. Analyzing this data provides valuable insights into customer preferences, travel patterns, and operational performance. This project uses Power BI to perform an in-depth analysis of Uber trip data, focusing on key aspects such as booking trends, trip frequencies, peak hours, vehicle usage, and location-based demand.

Objective

  • To analyze overall Uber trip bookings and identify growth trends.
  • To study time-based patterns such as peak booking hours, daily, weekly, and monthly demand.
  • To evaluate vehicle type preferences (e.g., UberX, UberXL, etc.) and their contribution to total trips.
  • To examine location-based insights such as top pickup and drop-off points.
  • To provide an interactive dashboard for stakeholders to explore data and derive actionable insights.

Methodology

  1. Data Collection.

    Obtained raw Uber trip data containing details such as booking date, time, vehicle type, pickup/drop-off locations, and fare amount.

  2. Data Cleaning.

    Removed duplicates, handled missing values, standardized formats (date/time, location), and eliminated inconsistencies.

  3. Data Transformation.

    Processed and structured the data using Power Query; created derived columns like day, month, hour, and trip duration for better analysis.

    DAX Calculations:

    Developed measures and KPIs (e.g., total trips, total revenue, average fare, trips per vehicle type, peak hour trips).

    Visualization:

    Designed interactive dashboards using bar charts, maps, line charts, and donut charts to represent patterns and comparisons.

Result

  1. The dataset contained details of Uber trips, including trip IDs, timestamps, pickup and drop-off locations, vehicle types, and fare amounts.
